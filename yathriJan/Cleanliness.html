Sanitation Laws

  • The Railways (Sanitary Conditions) Rules, 1992:
    These rules lay down the requirements for maintaining cleanliness and sanitation in railway premises, including trains and stations.
  • The Railway Servants (Duties, Hours of Work, Period of Rest, and Working Hours) Rules, 2005:
    These rules specify the duties of railway servants regarding cleanliness and sanitation.
  • The Indian Railways (Penalties for activities affecting cleanliness at railway premises) Rules, 2012:
    These rules provide for penalties for activities that affect cleanliness at railway premises.
  • The Railways (Maintenance of Cleanliness on Trains and at Railway Stations) Rules, 2013:
    These rules outline the responsibilities of railway authorities, passengers, and vendors for maintaining cleanliness on trains and at stations.
  • The Railway Servants (Health and Sanitation, Cleanliness and Safety) Rules, 2017:
    These rules specify the health and sanitation requirements for railway servants.
  • The Railway (Accommodation of Passengers) Rules, 2018:
    These rules lay down the standards for cleanliness and sanitation in railway accommodation provided to passengers.
  • The Railway (Prohibition of Spitting at Railway Premises) Rules, 2019:
    These rules prohibit spitting at railway premises and provide for penalties for violations.
  • The Railway (Collection and Disposal of Waste) Rules, 2020:
    These rules specify the procedures for the collection and disposal of waste at railway premises.
  • The Railway (Prohibition of Littering at Railway Premises) Rules, 2021:
    These rules prohibit littering at railway premises and provide for penalties for violations.
    • The Railway (Bio-medical Waste Management) Rules, 2022:
    These rules specify the procedures for the management of bio-medical waste generated at railway premises.

Cleanliness Guidelines for Passengers

  • Step 1: Personal Hygiene

    Maintain good personal hygiene by washing your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially before eating or touching your face.

  • Step 2: Use Hand Sanitizer

    Carry a hand sanitizer with you and use it regularly, especially after touching surfaces such as door handles or handrails.

  • Step 3: Wear a Mask

    Wear a mask covering your nose and mouth at all times while on the train or at the station, as directed by authorities.

  • Step 4: Keep Your Distance

    Maintain physical distance from other passengers, at least 6 feet, whenever possible.

  • Step 4: Cover Your Coughs and Sneezes

    Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when you cough or sneeze, and dispose of tissues properly.

  • Step 5: Avoid Touching Your Face

    Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands to prevent the spread of germs.

  • Step 6: Dispose of Trash Properly

    Use designated trash bins for disposing of trash and avoid littering on the train or at the station.

  • Step 7: Be Considerate of Others

    Be respectful of other passengers and railway staff by following all guidelines and instructions.

  • Step 8: Follow Public Health Advice

    Stay informed about the latest public health advice and guidelines regarding COVID-19 and other illnesses.

  • Step 9: Report Illness

    If you feel unwell during your journey, inform the railway staff immediately for assistance.

  • Step 10: Stay Updated

    Stay updated with the latest travel advisories and regulations related to cleanliness and hygiene.

Waste Management in Railways

Step 1: Segregation

Separate waste into categories such as recyclables, organic waste, and non-recyclables.

Step 2: Recycling

Establish recycling programs for materials like paper, plastics, and glass.

Step 3: Composting

Compost organic waste from catering services and food waste from passengers.

Step 4: Reducing Single-Use Plastics

Minimize the use of single-use plastics onboard trains and in railway stations.

Step 4: Waste Audits

Conduct regular waste audits to identify areas for improvement.

Step 5: Awareness Campaigns

Educate passengers and staff about the importance of waste management.

Step 6: Efficient Waste Collection

Ensure efficient waste collection systems are in place.

Step 7: Partnerships

Collaborate with local authorities, waste management companies, and NGOs.

Step 8: Innovation

Explore innovative technologies for waste management.
